## Overview

This repository presents a Lean Six Sigma case study focused on how Gentech, a multinational organization, applied process improvement principles to strengthen operational efficiency across its global business environment.

The project highlights a measurable improvement initiative centered on reducing cycle time, improving supply chain agility, and increasing cost-effectiveness through structured process analysis and continuous improvement methods.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to demonstrate how Lean Six Sigma can be used to:

- Reduce operational cycle time
- Improve process consistency and efficiency
- Strengthen supply chain responsiveness
- Support cost reduction and business agility

---

## Key Outcome

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Cycle time reduction | 15% |
| Primary focus | Operational efficiency |
| Business impact | Improved cost-effectiveness and agility |

> The repository states that Gentech reduced cycle time by **15%** while enhancing cost-effectiveness and supply chain agility.

## Project Scope

This project covers the application of Lean Six Sigma principles to a real-world business process improvement scenario. The emphasis is on:

- Identifying inefficiencies in the process flow
- Applying structured improvement methods
- Measuring impact through a clear performance outcome
- Communicating business value in a concise and executive-friendly format

---

## Methodology

### 1. Process Review
- Examine the current operating model
- Identify bottlenecks, delays, and waste
- Map the process from end to end

### 2. Lean Six Sigma Application
- Apply Lean principles to remove non-value-added activity
- Use Six Sigma thinking to improve consistency and reduce variation
- Focus on measurable improvement rather than theoretical change

### 3. Performance Improvement
- Reduce cycle time
- Improve process flow
- Increase operational agility
- Strengthen cost efficiency

### 4. Business Reporting
- Summarize the initiative in a clear project narrative
- Present the outcome in a way that is suitable for stakeholders and leadership
